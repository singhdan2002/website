If you are interested in learning more about Brutalist architecture, there are many resources available to help you deepen your understanding of this style.
Here are some recommended resources to get you started:

    Books:


  • "Concrete Concept: Brutalist Buildings Around the World" by Christopher Beanland: This book provides an overview of Brutalist architecture around the world, highlighting key buildings and the ideas behind the style.
  • "Brutal London" by Simon Phipps: Focusing specifically on Brutalist architecture in London, this book features stunning photography and detailed descriptions of some of the city's most iconic Brutalist buildings.
  • "SOS Brutalism: A Global Survey" by Oliver Elser and Philip Kurz: This book presents a comprehensive survey of Brutalist architecture around the world, featuring essays and photographs of over 120 buildings.

    Online resources:


  • The Brutalist Project: This online resource features a database of Brutalist buildings around the world, along with information and photos of each one.
  • ArchDaily: This architecture website features articles and photo galleries on a wide range of architectural styles, including Brutalism.

    Museums and exhibitions:


  • The Barbican Centre in London: This cultural center features a number of Brutalist buildings and offers tours, exhibitions, and events related to Brutalist architecture.
  • The National Building Museum in Washington, D.C.: This museum features a permanent exhibit on Brutalist architecture, as well as occasional special exhibitions on the topic.
  • The Canadian Centre for Architecture in Montreal: This museum and research center has an extensive collection of materials related to Brutalism, including photographs, drawings, and documents.
